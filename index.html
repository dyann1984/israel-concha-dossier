Israel Concha Built the Reintegration Infrastructure Mexico Needs

After living undocumented in the United States and experiencing deportation, return, violence, uncertainty, and the institutional gaps faced by millions of Mexican families, Israel Concha transformed his lived experience into New Comienzos, Little L.A., and Dream In Mexico — a binational reintegration infrastructure and ecosystem helping migrants, deported individuals, returnees, Dreamers, and binational families rebuild with dignity, opportunity, and structure.

Executive Summary

Executive Summary

Israel Concha is the founder of New Comienzos, a nonprofit organization serving migrants, deportees, returnees, Dreamers, and binational families through a growing binational ecosystem of support.

His lived experience became the foundation for a structured reintegration model integrating emotional support, legal orientation, Welcome Boxes, case management, mentorship, education, workforce development, housing and basic-needs assistance, technology-enabled services, referral systems, community organizing, media engagement, and cross-sector partnerships across the United States and Mexico.

Israel is also the founder of Little L.A. in Mexico City, a returnee and deportee community near the Monumento a la Revolución that has become a recognized example of migrant-led reintegration, entrepreneurship, cultural identity, and resilience.

Through Dream In Mexico by New Comienzos, Israel has built and continues to expand a practical reintegration pathway for people returning to Mexico. The Welcome Box serves as a first step into a larger support ecosystem, connecting participants to resources, mentorship, education, emotional support, legal referrals, and 90 days of personalized follow-up.

Israel Concha's work demonstrates how lived experience can be transformed into sustainable community infrastructure, practical reintegration systems, and a scalable framework for strengthening migrant reintegration across the United States and Mexico.

Origin Story

A Leader Formed by the Return Experience

Israel Concha's leadership was shaped by years of undocumented life in the United States, deportation, and forced return to Mexico — experiences shared by millions of Mexican families navigating migration, separation, and reintegration.

After returning to Mexico, Israel experienced firsthand the uncertainty, disconnection, and institutional gaps that many deported individuals and returnees face. Rather than allowing those experiences to define him, he transformed them into a lifelong mission of service, institution-building, and reintegration.

He began helping others navigate the same challenges by connecting them to emotional support, family reunification, employment, education, legal guidance, and the practical resources needed to rebuild their lives with dignity. Over time, those grassroots efforts evolved into a structured reintegration model supported by community partnerships, technology, education, workforce development, and cross-sector collaboration. That mission became New Comienzos.

Israel Concha's story is not only a story of return. It is a story of rebuilding, organizing, serving, and creating the infrastructure that did not exist when he needed it most.

Community Model

Little L.A.: A Living Model of Reintegration

In Mexico City's Tabacalera neighborhood, near the Monumento a la Revolución in the heart of downtown, Israel Concha helped build and strengthen what has become known as Little L.A. — a community where deportees, returnees, Dreamers, binational families, entrepreneurs, students, workers, and community leaders rebuild their lives after return.

Little L.A. is more than a neighborhood. It is a living laboratory for reintegration policy and community resilience.

Reintegration Platform

Dream In Mexico by New Comienzos

Dream In Mexico by New Comienzos is a structured reintegration platform designed to help Mexican returnees, deported individuals, Dreamers, and binational families rebuild their lives with dignity, opportunity, and long-term support.

Serving as the operational gateway to the New Comienzos reintegration ecosystem, the platform connects participants to Welcome Boxes, case management, mentorship, education, workforce pathways, emotional support, legal orientation, technology-enabled services, and personalized follow-up. Through a coordinated binational network spanning the United States and Mexico, Dream In Mexico transforms the return experience into a structured pathway toward stability, opportunity, and long-term reintegration.

The Welcome Box is the first point of contact with the New Comienzos reintegration ecosystem. Designed for Mexican returnees, deported individuals, Dreamers, and binational families, it provides practical guidance, immediate support, and a structured pathway toward long-term reintegration. Rather than a one-time donation, it connects participants to coordinated services across the ecosystem, including:

Structured Support

90 Days of Structure After Return

The 90-Day Reintegration Program provides personalized follow-up during one of the most vulnerable periods after return — helping participants move from crisis and uncertainty into stability, connection, and opportunity.

1

Intake

Initial intake, needs assessment, emotional support, and crisis orientation during the first days.

2

Stabilization

Legal orientation, education pathway, employment referrals, community connection, and mentorship.

3
Phase 3 · Growth

Development

Goal setting, case management, workforce readiness, English certification, and career development.

4
Phase 4 · Transition

Transition

Follow-up check-ins, transition plan, sustained community referrals, and long-term support pathways.

The first 90 days can determine whether a returnee falls through the cracks or begins rebuilding with dignity.

Executive Biography

Israel Concha is the Founder and Executive Director of New Comienzos, a binational nonprofit serving Mexican migrants, returnees, deported individuals, Dreamers, and binational families across the United States and Mexico. Over more than a decade of public service, he has led the development of the Dream In Mexico platform, the Welcome Box model, the 90-Day Reintegration Program, Little L.A., and a community-tested reintegration ecosystem built through direct service, institutional collaboration, research, technology, and innovation.
